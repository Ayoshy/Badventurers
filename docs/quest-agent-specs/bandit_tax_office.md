# Quest Spec: Bandit Tax Office

## Identity

- id: `bandit_tax_office`
- Titre FR: `Bureau des taxes bandit`
- Title EN: `Bandit Tax Office`
- Pitch FR: Des bandits ont compris qu'il etait plus rentable de voler les gens avec des formulaires, des files d'attente et une menace de controle fiscal. La guilde est engagee pour liberer les contribuables, recuperer l'or, et surtout comprendre pourquoi les bandits ont un tampon officiel.
- Pitch EN: Bandits discovered that forms, queue numbers, and audit threats make robbery look professional. The guild is hired to free the taxpayers, recover the gold, and figure out why the bandits own an official stamp.

## Recommended Tuning

- difficulty: `215`
- risk: `Medium`
- baseGold: `850`
- pityGold: `80`
- duration: `180` seconds
- partySlots: `3`
- Reward profile: gold-forward quest with standard loot rolls; failures should still pay enough to feel like "administrative compensation" rather than a dead run.
- Unlock fit: second or third repeatable quest, after `cave_minor_regrets`, once the player has seen the basic expedition/result loop.

## Best Hero Synergies

- `quill` / Bard Accountant: Best thematic fit. High Paperwork and Charisma let Quill weaponize receipts, calm angry taxpayers, and turn "miscellaneous fees" into bonus gold.
- `comptable` / Accountant: Premium paperwork specialist. Should get strong positive interactions on audit rooms, ledger puzzles, and tax-stamp checks.
- `ledger` / Sir Ledger: Legendary upside. Can transform a success into a great success flavor moment by finding the legal loophole the bandits were accidentally using.
- `mira` / Apprentice Mage: Good early accessible pick. High Magic and Paperwork support cursed filing cabinets, animated ledgers, and spellproof ink.
- `nell`, `vex`, or `sable` / Rogueish or Ninja: Sneak/Luck/Orientation help find hidden coin sacks, fake walls behind filing cabinets, and bandits disguised as clerks.
- `pax` / Priest and `chef_cuistot` / Chef: Hygiene/Charisma stabilizers. Useful for "public counter" encounters where the party needs to avoid a riot, a plague of ink, or both.
- `brugg` or `troll_stupide`: Can intimidate bandit guards, but should create comic risk around broken desks, destroyed evidence, and forms filled out in crayon.

## Expected Special Interactions

- Paperwork check: Heroes with high `Paperwork` can reduce risk, add bonus gold, or convert one failure journal line into a partial-success line.
- Counterfeit receipt cache: Heroes with high `Sneak`, `Luck`, or `Orientation` can find extra gold hidden in tax drawers, stamp boxes, or suspiciously thick rulebooks.
- Bad faith duel: Heroes with high `BadFaith` can argue with the bandit auditor until the auditor refunds themselves by mistake.
- Public counter calm: Heroes with high `Charisma` or `Hygiene` reduce the chance of "taxpayer riot" complications.
- Overconfident drawback: `Overconfident` heroes may sign something without reading it, producing a funny penalty or a smaller pity reward.
- ReadsManual upside: `ReadsManual` heroes can decode the office queue system and shorten result flavor around "correct desk, wrong century."
- PainfullyOrganized upside: `PainfullyOrganized` heroes can file the bandits under "assets seized" for bonus gold flavor.
- Demolition risk: `expert_en_demolition` may open the vault quickly, but can destroy proof of ownership, turning loot into "evidence dust."
- Salt interaction: `elementaire_de_sel` should react to taxes literally, salting ledgers until ink surrenders.

## Outcome Flavor Targets

- Great Success: The party exposes the whole fake tax racket, recovers extra gold, and accidentally receives an official refund.
- Success: The party clears the office, grabs the coin sacks, and leaves before anyone asks for duplicate copies.
- Partial Success: The party wins, but signs a minor form that costs time, dignity, or one suspicious service fee.
- Failure: The bandits keep most of the gold, but the guild receives pity pay as "consulting expenses."
- Ridiculous Failure: The party becomes temporarily employed by the tax office and is paid a tiny severance after being fired for competence.
